What we can validate

Usability is not a matter of opinion.

Good testing answers a specific question: can a user find the key function, understand the navigation, complete a task and tell what just happened?

▣

Applications and websites

Mobile and desktop interfaces, information architecture, navigation, forms and key user journeys.

◇

Services and processes

The whole user experience across touchpoints, from the first piece of information to completing the service.

◎

Emerging interactions

Digital assistants, unconventional interfaces, VR/AR and prototypes for which established patterns do not yet exist.

How collaboration works

From a question to an actionable recommendation.

The scope adapts to the project. The aim is not a long report, but evidence that supports the next design decision.

1

Research question

We define what you need to learn and which decision the study should support.

2

Tasks and participants

We prepare realistic tasks and recruit people who match the target group.

3

Testing sessions

We observe behaviour, problems and user strategies and ask questions at the right moment.

4

Findings and priorities

Results become specific, prioritised recommendations for the next iteration.

Who UXLab is for

You can test before everything is finished.

The earlier a major issue is found, the less expensive it is to fix. A sketch, clickable prototype or live product can all be meaningfully evaluated.
