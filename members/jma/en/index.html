System, rules, player

A game idea must work as a functioning system.

Jan connects software engineering with practical game development. His teaching covers game-engine architecture, mechanic implementation, procedural content and the design of physical and digital game experiences.

01

Engines and architecture

Game-engine structure, components, design patterns, physics, graphics, artificial intelligence and multiplayer.

02

Procedural systems

Generating dungeons, maps and game content, managing difficulty and designing extensible game modules.

03

Escape rooms and puzzles

Puzzle construction, puzzle graphs, narrative pacing, prototyping and validating the experience with players.

From principle to prototype

Teaching that ends with a working game.

Technical topics lead into practical projects in Unity, Godot and other engines, which students develop through semester projects and theses.
